How the methods work +
Reference ET comes from ERA5-Land with the FAO-56 Penman Monteith equation. The FAO-56 method multiplies it by a crop coefficient that changes through the growth stages. The NDVI method derives the coefficient from satellite greenness (Sentinel-2, gap-filled with MODIS), so it follows the actual canopy.
Reading the output +
The total is the water need over the whole season or period; the mean is the daily average. The chart compares reference ET with crop ET. The map shows the mean crop ET over the field.
